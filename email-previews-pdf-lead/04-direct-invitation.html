Ó COMÁIN

A note from the Office

A place for you in the Register at Newhall

Dear Aoife,

The Chief has asked me to write to you directly.

There is a place for you in the Register of Clan Ó Comáin, if you would like to take it. Whatever surname you carry, and whether or not your line runs into ours, you are welcome here. The Chief will enter your name with his own hand, in the form you choose, and will set his seal upon a certificate to be sent to you in the post. Membership opens at €49 the year (€79 family), and the entry can be made today.

Take a place in the Register →

I should mention that the kindred coming to Ó Comáin in the present revival are writing in from Boston, Chicago, the cities of Britain, Sydney, Auckland, and the towns of Ireland itself — people of every surname who simply love Ireland and want a real place in its living Gaelic story. Your name would stand among them.

There is also one matter the Chief has asked me to draw to your attention. 2026 is Year One of the Revival. Members who join this year are recorded in the clan Register as Founders of the Revival — a distinction that appears on the certificate as a gold seal, stays with the record for the lifetime of the clan, and can only be earned this year. Once 2026 closes, that distinction will not be conferred again.

If you would consider one of the higher tiers — Guardian for the public Register and the Chief's Black Tie Gala at Newhall House, Life Member for the standing in perpetuity — I am happy to answer any question you might have. Please do write back.

Take a place in the Register →

With kind regards,

Linda Commane Cryan

Private Secretary to the Chief of Ó Comáin
