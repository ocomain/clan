Ó COMÁIN

A note from the Office

On the certificate, and what membership carries

Dear Aoife,

A further note from me, with a little more about what is conferred upon those who take a place in the Register at Newhall.

Each member receives a certificate of membership signed by the Chief, by his own hand, bearing the arms of Ó Comáin and the seal of the clan. It is the formal instrument by which the Chief confers a place upon the holder. Members frame it and keep it on the wall — many tell us it is among the most cherished things in the family home.

I should mention something the Chief considers important. The certificate names the holder, and — for those who join as a family — names the spouse and the children, with an inheritance clause by which the children carry their place in the clan into adulthood in their own right. One certificate. One Irish clan. One family, three generations deep. It is the most enduring Irish heirloom we know how to make.

The four tiers of membership are these:

Clan Member — €49 individual, €79 family. Entry into the Register at Newhall. The certificate signed and sealed by the Chief. Chief-approved use of the clan crest and the official clan tartan. Member access to the summer festivals at Newhall and Cahercommane.

Guardian of the Clan — €150 individual, €220 family. All the foregoing, with the certificate posted as a physical document on heritage stock. Your name on the public Founding Members Register at Newhall. A personal letter from the Chief on headed Newhall stationery. A place at the table at the private annual Guardians dinner at Newhall House, with the Chief and Privy Council. First right of refusal on open Privy Council positions.

Steward of the Clan — €350 individual, €480 family. All the foregoing, with the standing of a Steward of the kindred, your name inscribed on the Clan Roll of Honour permanently displayed at Newhall, your line of descent lodged in the clan archive, and a private call with the Chief himself.

Life Member — €750 individual, €1,100 family, paid once. All the foregoing, conferred for life with no further annual renewal. A clan heirloom pack presented as a keepsake set. Your name engraved permanently on the Clan Roll of Honour at Newhall.

Membership is, as the Chief puts it, an act of cultural stewardship — not a subscription. It directly funds the clan's revival: the summer festivals, the academic and heritage research, the preservation of Cahercommane, and the books and publications carrying the ancient Irish Gaelic story to new readers.

With kind regards,

Linda Commane Cryan

Private Secretary to the Chief of Ó Comáin
