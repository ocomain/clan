Patron saints

The saints of our name

The Ó Comáin name is bound to two great Irish saints whose legacies are written in stone, in place names and in the living tradition of the Irish church. Both bear the Commán name and both left foundations that endure to this day.

Patron saint

Saint Commán of Roscommon

Died 747 AD · Annals of Ulster

Saint Commán is the founder of Roscommon — Ros Commáin, meaning "Commán's Wood" — and patron saint of County Roscommon, whose name the county and town carry to this day. His death is recorded in the Annals of Ulster at 747 AD, making him one of the most firmly dateable saints of early medieval Ireland.

Saint Commán is also documented as founder of the ancient church at Kinvara in County Galway, on the southern edge of the Burren — the very heartland of the ancient Chiefdom of Tulach Commáin, and within sight of the ancestral capital Cahercommaun. His foundation at Kinvara places the spiritual legacy of the Comáin name directly within the clan's ancestral landscape.

The Dictionary of National Biography (1887) records that Commán "also founded the church of Ceann Mara, now Kinvara." His feast day is celebrated in the Roscommon tradition, and his memory is preserved in the dedicated book Remembering St. Comán — Patron Saint of Ros Comáin by Noel Hoare, published by the Rathcroghan Heritage Centre.

Patron saint

Saint Commán of Kinvara

c. 6th–7th century · Burren, Co. Galway

Saint Commán of Kinvara founded the ancient church at Kinvara on the southern shore of Galway Bay, at the edge of the Burren — just a few miles from the ancestral chiefdom capital of Cahercommaun. This geographical proximity between the saint's foundation and the clan's ancestral capital is a remarkable convergence of the spiritual and political history of the Comáin name.

Kinvara — Cinn Mhara, meaning "head of the sea" — was one of the principal ecclesiastical sites of the Burren region in the early medieval period. The saint's church there would have served the very community whose chief gave the chiefdom its name. Whether Saint Commán of Kinvara and Saint Commán of Roscommon are the same person or two distinct individuals remains a matter of scholarly discussion — the Dictionary of National Biography treats them as one.

The Grokipedia article on Saint Commán of Kinvara provides a valuable summary of what is known about this saint and his connection to the Burren landscape and the Comáin heritage.

In memoriam

Ronan James Patrick Commane

1989 — 2023

His greatest joy was making others happy.
His philosophy: love.

Ronan's love for his extended family, and for all people, inspired the rebirth of Clan Ó Comáin. It is in his memory and in his spirit of joy and connection that this clan has been revived — bringing together the descendants and friends of the Ó Comáin name from across the world.

Ronan was the second son of Fergus Commane, Chief of Clan Ó Comáin. He passed to his eternal reward in 2023. He is remembered in every gathering of this clan, and in every act of welcome extended to those who join us.

Ar dheis Dé go raibh a anam
May his soul be at God's right hand
