Wondering whether the clan is for you?

The bond is what matters, not the bloodline. All who answer the call are welcome.

This is not a modern softening of an ancient idea — it is a return to one. The Gaelic clan was never purely a matter of blood. Every clan kept its céile — sworn clients who pledged service to the chief — and its muintir, the wider household of those who lived under his protection. Foster-bonds, surety-bonds, and chosen allegiance were all recognised ways of belonging. A man's place in a clan could be earned as well as inherited, and the chief's word was what made it real.

That older way is the way Clan Ó Comáin welcomes its members today. All are entered into the same Register, signed by the same Chief, recognised by the same Council. What matters is the bond you choose to make.

Common questions

Everything you need to know

Do I need to have an Irish surname to join?
No. Clan Ó Comáin is open to all who love Ireland and its ancient Gaelic culture — whatever name you carry. In Brehon law, clans always admitted members through shared values and commitment, not bloodline alone.
What does the physical certificate look like?
The certificate is a formal document on high-quality stock, bearing the clan arms and signed by the Chief. It is a keepsake document designed to be framed and displayed.
When are the summer festivals at Newhall?
Dates are announced to registered members first, ahead of any public announcement. Festivals take place at Newhall Estate and Cahercommane in the Burren — the clan's ancestral capital in County Clare.
What does family membership cover?
Family membership covers two named adults and all children under 18. Both adults are named on the certificate and the clan register. Children are included automatically and inherit the family's membership status.
Can I upgrade my membership tier later?
Yes. You can move to a higher tier at any time — simply apply again and the difference will be calculated. Life membership can be applied for at any point by existing annual members.
What is the Chief's Black Tie Gala Dinner at Newhall House?
An annual dinner held at Newhall House — the seat of the Chief — for Guardian, Steward and Life members of the clan. Hosted by the Chief and members of the Privy Council. Numbers are strictly limited by the capacity of the house. As is customary at private clan functions, members contribute to the cost of the evening.
Is payment secure?
Payment is processed via Stripe — the industry standard for secure online payments, used by millions of organisations worldwide. Your card details are never stored by the clan.
What if I want to cancel?
Annual memberships can be cancelled at any time before renewal. There are no penalties and no questions asked. Life memberships are non-refundable by their nature — they are a permanent commitment.
