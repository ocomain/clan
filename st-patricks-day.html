An old day, kept across the diaspora.

Wherever the name went after the dispossession — to America, to Britain, to Australia, to Canada — Patrick's Day was the day the name remembered itself. Sixteen centuries on, the family still threads through the diaspora, and every March 17th, in pubs from Ennis to Toronto, the kindred lift a glass to the long memory of the clan.
