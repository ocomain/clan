Commons - Commins - O Comain - County Clare

The Commons and Commins family -- an ancient Gaelic name in the Irish landscape

Commons and Commins are rare surviving forms of the Gaelic O Comain -- a name embedded in the landscape of Clare since the 8th century.

The origin of Commons and Commins in Ireland

The surnames Commons and Commins are among the rarer surviving anglicised forms of the ancient Irish Gaelic name O Comain. In the Griffiths Valuation of 1847-1864, 360 families were recorded as Commons and 150 as Commins across Ireland -- concentrated in Munster and Connacht, the heartland of the ancient O Comain clan.

The -s suffix in Commons and Commins carries the same meaning as the O prefix in Irish: descendant of. These are the same family under different anglicisations. Edward MacLysaght confirmed the connection explicitly in The Surnames of Ireland (Irish Academic Press, 1985) -- Commons and Commins represent O Comain, the ancient Gaelic name of a clan whose documented history stretches from 658 AD to the present day.

Because Commons and Commins are rarer than Cummins, many families carrying these names have been unaware of their connection to one of County Clare's most ancient Gaelic lineages. The clan's revival offers an opportunity to reclaim that history.

The Commons and Commins names are rare precisely because they preserve the name more faithfully than the more common Cummins. If you carry either spelling, your connection to Clan O Comain is direct and documented.

One name, one ancient lineage

Whether spelled Commons, Commins, Cummins, Commane or O Comain -- these are all anglicised forms of the same Gaelic name, from the same ancient clan. The ancestor who gives the surname its meaning is Coman mac Breanan Dall, who lived around 620-650 AD, son of Breanan Dall, 12th King of Ui Maine -- recorded in the Annals of Ulster.

The clan held the Chiefdom of Tulach Commain in the Burren of County Clare from the 8th century. The great stone fort of Cahercommane -- the ceremonial inauguration site of the chiefs -- still bears the family name in the Clare landscape today.

About the clan

Clan O Comain is an ancient Gaelic royal house, officially recognised by Clans of Ireland in 2025. The clan traces its documented history to 658 AD, with DNA evidence stretching 4,000 years. Membership is open to all who love Ireland and wish to protect its ancient Gaelic culture.
