The Library

Stories and history from Gaelic Ireland — Brehon law, the clan system, the saints, the manuscripts, the language, the folklore. The long inheritance, told carefully.

A growing collection of pieces on the deep heritage of Ireland. Not blog posts. Reference articles meant to be returned to — to share, to send on, to settle an argument. Each one rooted in primary sources where they survive, in the careful work of historians where they don't, and in the living memory the clan still carries.

Kept by the Seanchaí
Paddy Commane
In the Gaelic tradition, the seanchaí was the keeper of memory — the one who held the stories, the laws and the lore of the people, and told them aloud so they would not be lost. Paddy Commane carries that office for Clan Ó Comáin. When he tells, he becomes the tale — and these are his tellings.
