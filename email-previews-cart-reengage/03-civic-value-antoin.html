Ó COMÁIN

A word from the Tánaiste

What your hand on this work would mean

Dear Aoife,

Antoin Commane writing — Tánaiste of Clan Ó Comáin. The role is the elected successor to the Chief in the Brehon line, and one part of it is to speak for what the clan is making of itself in the years to come. The Office has told me your application stands unfinished, and the Chief has asked that I write to you on this.

I want to put one thing plainly, because it is the part that does not always come across in the form-filling.

Membership of Clan Ó Comáin is an act of cultural stewardship, not a subscription. The €49 of the first tier — and the €150 of the Guardian, and the €750 of Life — does not buy a service. It funds the work of an Irish Gaelic clan in active revival. The summer festivals at Newhall and Cahercommane. The academic and heritage research. The preservation of the fort itself, which is on no one's budget but ours. The Chronicle and the publications carrying the ancient story to new readers. The Privy Council's standing meetings. The seal on every certificate. None of this happens without the kindred's hand on it.

If your application sits unfinished because the question was is it worth €49 — the truer question is what your hand on this work would mean, and what it could enable for the clan in the years your name appears beside ours.

The clan has work to do. Your hand on it would matter.
