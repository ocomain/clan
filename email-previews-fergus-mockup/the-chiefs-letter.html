Notes for the Council & Chief — not part of the email

Chancery stamp redrawn — Royal House of Georgia style. The previous wax-seal version felt theme-park; the new stamp is the genuine register: red ink, two concentric rings, ‘THE COMMANE · CHIEF OF Ó COMÁIN’ curving across the top, ‘XXVI BARON SINCE MCCCXXX’ curving across the bottom, small star ornaments left and right separating the two arcs, the actual heraldic arms rendered as red line-art in the centre, slight ink-break imperfections to feel hand-stamped. Larger and positioned to slightly overlap the signature, with a small clockwise tilt — exactly how a real chancery stamp would be pressed onto outgoing correspondence.

Body in serif (placeholder for handwritten version). The body is currently set in Times New Roman / EB Garamond serif. The plan is to commission a professional calligrapher (e.g., Fiverr) to write the Chief's letter in real ink on real paper, scan it as a high-resolution PNG, and use that image as the body. That will give the genuine ink-on-paper feel that a web font cannot match. Until then, the serif body shows the layout faithfully.

Why no ‘Caveat’ cursive font. The earlier mockup used a Google Font (‘Caveat’) for handwriting — confirmed too web-y/tacky for the dignity of the correspondence. A real calligrapher's scanned hand will read miles better.

Reference. Aesthetic and stamp positioning modelled on Royal House of Georgia chancery correspondence.
