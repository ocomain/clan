Newhall Estate · County Clare · Ireland

Dear Aoife,

A short word from Newhall, by my own hand.

It is the custom of the Office to send the formalities — the certificate, the seal, the entry in the Register. I wanted to write to you directly, beyond all of that, simply to say that I am glad you are here.

The clan came near to vanishing. Between the Penal years, the Famine, and the great emigrations that followed, the line frayed almost to nothing. That you and others are choosing to gather back to it now — from across the world, across the surnames the Anglicising clerks scattered, across the long centuries — is the slow undoing of that long unmaking.

Whatever brought you to the door, you are welcome at it. Whatever your line, whatever name you carry, your place in the Register is real and the seal upon your certificate is set by my own hand.

Linda and the Office shall write to you in the weeks to come on the matters that need attending to. From me, only this: there is a house in County Clare where the lights are on, and you have a place at it.

Yours, in clan and kindred,

Fergus Commane Kinfauns
