Dear Aoife,

It is my pleasure to welcome you to the newly revived Clan Ó Comáin!

After a long suppression of 800 years, the clan is back, and I am thrilled that you have chosen to be a part of our founding family in 2026.

I am incredibly excited to grow our community and would love for you to help & support me and be a part of that journey. I encourage you to browse our website to learn more about our shared heritage, the fascinating history of Irish clans, and how our ancestors lived under Brehon Law. Don't forget to explore the special Private Members' area.

Here’s to a long and wonderful friendship!

Slán go fóill — (goodbye for now)

Fergus Kinfauns, The Commane

Chief of Ó Comáin

P.S. “Know someone who belongs with us? Inviting them is easy through your members' area!”

Notes for the Council & Chief — not part of the email

What is new in this revision. Header subline ‘An Ancient Gaelic Royal House’ restored. Sign-off ‘Fergus Kinfauns, The Commane’ now in a single font with no italic-gold treatment. Wax seal redrawn: just the mermaid (no harp), with ‘XXVI’ Roman numeral over her and ‘XXVI BARON SINCE MCCCXXX’ legend curving around the top edge — a subtle reference to the Chief's 26th Lord Kinfauns Scottish title held since 1330. Body letter is the Chief's own words, set in a handwriting font (‘Caveat’) to feel like a real personal letter rather than typed correspondence.

The handwriting font. ‘Caveat’ from Google Fonts — refined, slightly irregular, reads naturally at large sizes. Falls back to Bradley Hand / cursive on clients that block web fonts. If the Chief prefers a different cursive face we can swap (e.g., ‘Homemade Apple’ for a more rounded hand, ‘Dancing Script’ for sharper, or generate the body as an image for true ink-on-paper feel).

The famous PS. Set off with a thin gold left-rule and a small-caps ‘P.S.’ eyebrow above, carrying through the same handwriting font as the body. Distinct but visually continuous.
