Ó COMÁIN

By the Chief’s hand · A raising in rank

You are raised to Ardchara

Dia dhuit, Ardchara Antoin — God be with you.

Five souls have come to Clan Ó Comáin through your welcome. The Chief has marked this, and has been pleased to raise you from the dignity of Cara to that of Ardchara — the second honour in the keeping of the clan. Where Cara names the friend, Ardchara names the high friend — the clan’s recognition that you carry the work of welcome with particular grace.

It pleases the Chief to raise you from Cara to the dignity of

Ardchara

ARD-khar-uh — Friend of high standing

To bring five is the work of a member who has made the clan their own. The Chief is the better for knowing it.

The dignity of Cara, lately held, is laid by; the dignity of Ardchara is taken up in its place.

Your title is held quietly with the Chief and the Herald — recorded in the clan’s keeping at Newhall. There is no fanfare to it; only the recognition of what you have done, in the company of those who do likewise.
