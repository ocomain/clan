Ó COMÁIN

A note from Newhall

Your year with the clan

Dia dhuit, Antoin — God be with you.

It is almost a year since Maeve O'Connor set a place for you in Clan Ó Comáin. Your gift membership was a one-year clan member, and it will quietly come to its end on 23 June 2026 — at which point your name will move from active standing into the keeping of the clan archive.

We wanted to write in good time — so that, if you would like to remain in current standing on your own terms, there is space to decide without pressure.

A year is enough to learn what Ó Comáin feels like from the inside. What comes next is yours to shape.

If you would like to continue, the door remains open. You are welcome to take up any tier that fits — year by year, or for life. Continuing your membership keeps your name in current standing on the Register, and your founder distinction (carried by your 2026 certificate) alongside it. A further small thing: from a year and a day on the Register, you also pass from Observer to Standing Member of Clan Ó Comáin, with voice and vote at the Annual General Meeting.

Continue your membership →

And if now is not the time — that is also all right. Your certificate remains yours, and the clan keeps a record of every name it has called its own. You are welcome back at any point you wish to return to the Register in current standing. If you have any questions, write to clan@ocomain.org and I will respond on behalf of the Chief.

Go raibh míle maith agat for the year you have given the clan.

Linda Commane Cryan
