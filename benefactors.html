Benefactors

For those who wish to fund the work.

Membership joins you to the kindred. Benefaction underwrites the work the kindred does — the conservation of ancient sites, the pedigree research, the festivals, the slow rebuilding of a Gaelic house that was nearly lost.

The work

The revival is held up by particular work in particular places.

Clan Ó Comáin holds responsibility for some of the oldest continuously remembered sites in Munster and Connacht. The work is concrete: the conservation of Killone Abbey — a twelfth-century Augustinian priory at Newhall — and of Cahercommane, the stone ringfort that bears the clan's name. The scholarly work of recovering the clan pedigree from the Book of Lecan and the medieval annals, and the building of a permanent archive at Newhall in which that work can rest.

Alongside this, the genetic record — the Y-DNA project that has placed the clan's deep ancestry in ancient Ireland — and the festivals at Newhall that bring the diaspora home. Each year the Office of the Clan publishes what has been funded and what has been done.

Benefactors choose what their contribution funds. The Office writes to each in the autumn with the year's planned work; the Benefactor names the piece of work to be associated with their patronage. A Benefactor of the conservation at Killone is recorded as such; a Benefactor of the pedigree research, as such.

The three standings

Benefactor, Great Benefactor, Protector of the Clan.

Each contribution is a single act, made once, in a given year — not a subscription. A Benefactor may renew their patronage in a future year if they wish; the Office writes to each Benefactor in the autumn to invite a fresh act of patronage for the year ahead.

Tíolacthóir
Benefactor of the Clan
€5,000
one act · one year

The Benefactor of the Clan underwrites a named piece of work for the year — the digitisation of a manuscript, a conservation survey at Cahercommane or Killone, a year's research grant, the printing of a clan publication. The Office and the Benefactor agree the piece of work together.

Recognition. Named (with consent) in the year's published report as Benefactor of the named work. A personal letter of thanks from the Chief, written by hand, on clan headed stationery.
Tíolacthóir Mór
Great Benefactor of the Clan
€15,000
one act · one year

The Great Benefactor underwrites a year of a stewardship — the archive at Newhall, the pedigree research programme, the conservation works at one of the named sites, the summer festival. Named permanently against the work that was funded.

Recognition. Named permanently against the funded stewardship in the clan archive at Newhall. An annual letter from the Chief. An invitation to visit Newhall as a guest of the clan when the year's work is shown.
Coimirceoir an Tí
Protector of the Clan
€50,000
one act · legacy standing

In the older Brehon tradition, a Coimirceoir was a noble who took a kindred under formal protection — a relationship between two houses, the elder underwriting the work of the younger. The standing is rare by design: a Protector of the Clan at Clan Ó Comáin underwrites the revival at a scale that supports the whole arc of a year's work, not a single piece of it.

The Protector and the Chief together set the priorities for what the year's work shall be.

Recognition. Named permanently in the Coimirceoirí Register at Newhall — a record kept in perpetuity, alongside the clan's own pedigree. An annual private conversation with the Chief. A standing welcome at Newhall. The Office holds these relationships personally; there are no more than a small number in any year.
Or, by bequeathal
Benefaction by bequeathal — recognised now, settled in time.

A Benefactor may commit to any of the three standings by bequeathal — the contribution agreed in writing now, settled from the patron's estate in the fullness of time. The patron's standing as a Benefactor is recognised from the date of the agreement, not the date of settlement. The clan's recognition arrives in the patron's lifetime; the work it funds is the work the clan shall do in the year of their final settlement, named in their honour.

A patron may also combine an immediate Benefaction with a bequeathal — making an act of patronage today, and committing to a further act from their estate. Many do. The terms are agreed privately with the Office of the Chancellor.

A final settlement
To remember the clan in your estate, without prior act.

Some friends of the clan, particularly those undertaking their own estate planning, wish simply to be remembered in their final settlement — without making any present-day act of Benefaction. Such gifts are received with the same care. Write privately to the Office of the Chancellor; matters are handled discreetly, in accordance with your wishes, at your own pace.

To begin

A conversation, not a transaction.

Patronage at these levels begins with a letter. The Office of the Chancellor handles each conversation personally — agreeing what the contribution shall fund, the form of recognition, and the manner of payment.
