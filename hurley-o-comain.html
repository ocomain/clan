Hurley - O Comain - Clare and Cork

The Hurley name and its hidden Gaelic connection

In parts of Clare and Cork, Hurley is not O hUirthile -- it is O Comain, misrecorded by priests in the early 19th century. The clan welcomes all Hurley families.

How Hurley became O Comain's forgotten variant

The surname Hurley in Ireland derives from several distinct Gaelic origins. Most Hurleys descend from O hUirthile or O Muirthile -- native Clare and Cork families. But a significant number of Hurley families in Counties Clare and Cork carry a name that was originally O Comain -- recorded as Hurley by priests who mistakenly believed the name derived from caman, the Irish word for a hurling stick.

This practice -- known in Irish genealogy as pseudo-translation -- was documented by Edward MacLysaght, the first Chief Herald of Ireland, who wrote: O Comain has become Hurley in some parts of Cos. Clare and Cork, due to the mistaken belief that it derives from caman, a hurley. Genealogist Robert Matheson recorded cases from the early 19th century where a marriage certificate would record a woman as Mary Hurley in the priest's hand, while her own signature beneath read Mary Commane.

If your Hurley family comes from Clare or north Cork -- particularly around Killone, Kilmihil or the Burren -- there is a real possibility your name was originally O Comain.

Frank McNally of The Irish Times documented the Hurley / O Comain confusion in his piece Synonyms of the Fathers -- a phenomenon that separated families from their true name in the documentary record. This clan exists, in part, to restore that connection.

All Hurleys are welcome in Clan O Comain

Whatever the origin of your Hurley name -- whether it is O Comain misrecorded, or O hUirthile of Clare, or O Muirthile of Cork -- Clan O Comain is open to all. In Brehon law, the clan was always bigger than any single bloodline. Membership is open to all who love Ireland and its ancient Gaelic heritage.

For those with Clare Hurley ancestry specifically, the clan's ancestral lands at Newhall and the Chiefdom of Tulach Commain in the Burren represent a shared landscape -- a shared story -- that connects all the ancient families of north Clare across more than a thousand years.

About the clan

Clan O Comain is an ancient Gaelic royal house, officially recognised by Clans of Ireland in 2025. The clan traces its documented history to 658 AD, with DNA evidence stretching 4,000 years. Membership is open to all who love Ireland and wish to protect its ancient Gaelic culture.
