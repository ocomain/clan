CLAN Ó COMÁIN

Email correspondence — for review

A complete catalogue of the household's automated email sequences, prepared for the Council and the Chief.

What this is. Three email sequences are now in place across the clan's communications infrastructure, totalling eighteen distinct emails. Each is voiced by a specific member of the household — Linda, the Chief, the Herald, Paddy as Seanchaí, Antoin as Tánaiste — and triggered by a particular moment in a member's or prospect's relationship with the clan.

What we ask. Read the emails as the recipient would receive them. Click any title below to view the rendered email exactly as it would land in an inbox. Edits, refinements, and reworkings are welcomed — every email here can be adjusted before any further volume goes out.

One note on the cart re-engagement sequence. Of the three, that sequence is the most recent and has had the least review. The Council's eye on Antoin's voice and Paddy's "is the clan real" framing in particular would be valuable.

Sequence one

Founder invitation

For people Fergus has personally identified as founding contacts of the clan. Sent by the Office on his behalf, one-to-one.

2 emails over a year. Voice: The Herald, on behalf of Fergus. Trigger: manual send via the admin panel; reminder via daily-gift-acceptance-sweep.
Day 0

The Herald, on behalf of Fergus — A founding place

The original invitation. Names the recipient, frames the year-ahead membership as the Chief's gift, single CTA to claim the place. Optional one-line personal note from Fergus shown above the body when Fergus typed one in the admin form.
+30 d

The Herald — A final notice, your founding place in Clan Ó Comáin

The final touch, fired only if the recipient has not claimed by day 30. Frames the founding-place gift as a one-time courtesy that is not re-offered, with the standard Clan Ó Comáin membership remaining open as the future path. Two CTAs: take up the place, or visit the clan website. After this no further messages are sent.

Sequence two

Member invitation

For people a clan member personally recommends for membership. Sent on the member's behalf, with their name throughout, framed as a friend's vouch rather than a pitch.

1 email. Voice: The Herald, written for and on behalf of the inviting member. Trigger: a member submits the invitation form in the members' area.
Day 0

The Herald — by private welcome to an Irish clan

Names the inviting member throughout. Three short paragraphs: the inviter has thought of you among 'the small number they would wish to see standing with them' → what Clan Ó Comáin is (Gaelic royal house, presidential patronage, Clans of Ireland) → 'door, opened, and a friend on the other side'. Optional one-line personal note from the inviter shown above the body when they typed one.

Sequence three

Gift flows

Emails sent around a gift purchase — to the buyer (their receipt and a later keepsake), and to the recipient (welcome to the clan).

6 emails across the gift lifecycle. Voice: Linda (Office of the Private Secretary). Triggers: Stripe webhook on gift purchase; cert-publication when the recipient seals their certificate.
Buyer · Day 0

Linda — Your gift is on its way (deferred-acceptance)

Sent to the buyer post-purchase when the recipient is new to the clan and must claim their place. Sets correct expectations: the gift is held one year, acceptance is the recipient's act, the buyer's sponsorship credit is awarded immediately on payment regardless. Includes a quiet 'tell them the email is on its way' nudge to reduce inbox loss.
Buyer · Day 0

Linda — Your gift is on its way (existing-member tier upgrade)

Variant for when the recipient is already a clan member and the gift is a tier upgrade. No 'claim my place' step; existing-member welcome path. The 30-day cert-refinement window applies.
Recipient · Day 0

Linda — A gift to you from {giver} (deferred)

For new-to-clan recipients. Frames the gift as 'freely given' with 'no obligation'. Single CTA: 'View your invitation' → claim flow on /gift-welcome.html. The membership materialises only when they press claim. Quotes the giver's personal message if one was written.
Recipient · Day 0

Linda — A gift to you from {giver} (tier upgrade)

Variant for when the recipient is already a clan member and this gift is a tier upgrade. Routes to the cert publication flow with the standard 30-day refinement window. One-click magic-link sign-in via the buyer's existing email.
Buyer · Day 0

Linda — Gift confirmed (alt path)

A shorter buyer-confirmation variant that still reaches some flows. Lighter than #1: just 'Gift confirmed, the office will be in touch with your recipient'. Worth reviewing for whether this should be retired in favour of the fuller version above.
Buyer · Later

Linda — {recipient} has been welcomed (keepsake)

Sent to the gift buyer after the recipient publishes their certificate, often weeks later. Includes a copy of the published certificate as PDF attachment — a souvenir of the gift given. Closes the loop on the gift transaction.

Sequence seven

Transactional notifications

One-off receipts, confirmations, and audit emails that fire in response to specific actions — purchases, cert publications, sponsor conversions, threshold crossings. Less editorial weight than the campaign sequences (these are notifications, not nurture), but still part of the voice of the clan.

9 emails. Voice: The Herald (chivalric warrant register for sponsorship work), Linda (Office, for direct receipts and confirmations). Triggers: Stripe webhook (purchase, abandonment), cert-publication flow, day-29 reminder sweep, sponsor conversion service.
Day 0

Linda — Céad míle fáilte (direct buyer welcome)

The most-seen email in the system. Every paid signup gets this: post-purchase welcome, your tier and benefits, single-click magic-link to confirm certificate details. Linda signs as Office of the Private Secretary; Fergus is named in the body as the Chief on whose behalf this is sent.
On publish

Linda — Your certificate is sealed

Sent the moment a member presses the seal on their certificate. PDF attached, signed download URL valid 30 days, audit trail in the Register. The emotional moment of the cert becoming real.
Day 30

Linda — Your certificate, now sealed (auto-published variant)

Same email, different framing: when the member's 30-day cert-refinement window closes without action, the cert is auto-issued in their name as it stands. Includes a quiet offer that re-issues are at the Chief's discretion if details should have been refined.
Day 29

Linda — Your certificate seals tomorrow

Final 24-hour reminder for members who haven't sealed their certificate yet. Shows the current draft details — name (with auto-correction flagged if applicable), tier, family on certificate, ancestor dedication — so the member can refine before the +30 auto-seal runs. One-click magic-link sign-in into the dashboard publish flow. If the member does nothing, the day-30 auto-seal handles it and they get the auto-sealed variant above.
On conversion

The Herald — {newMember} has taken their place, through you

Sent to a sponsor whenever one of their invitees converts to membership. Title-aware greeting (addresses the sponsor by their current dignity if they hold one). Short, warm, the Herald's voice on behalf of the Chief.
On 1st conversion

The Herald — Raised to the dignity of Cara

First raising into honour. Sent to a sponsor when their first invitee converts. Chivalric warrant register: 'It pleases the Chief to confer upon you'. Sets the tone for the sponsorship ladder.
On 5th conversion

The Herald — Raised from Cara to the dignity of Ardchara

Threshold raising: from Cara ('friend') to Ardchara ('high friend'). Replacement model — the prior dignity is laid by, the new one taken up. Measured raising-in-rank tone, friendship-tier deepening.
On 15th conversion

The Herald — Raised from Ardchara to the dignity of Onóir

The apex of the sponsorship ladder. Formal 'It hath pleased' chivalric warrant of advancement to the highest rank. Henceforth-clause and place-and-standing privilege-clause. The register shifts from friendship-tier to ceremonial honour.
+1 d

Linda — Your place in Clan Ó Comáin is still open

For applicants who started checkout but didn't complete payment. Frames the year-one Founding Members designation as a reason to come back, not as pressure. Single 'Complete your membership' CTA; explicit invitation to write if something went wrong.
