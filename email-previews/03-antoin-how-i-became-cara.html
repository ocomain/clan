Ó COMÁIN

A note from the Tánaiste

How I became Cara

Dear Aoife,

Antoin Commane writing. The Chief has asked me to write to you on a particular matter, but not in my Tánaiste capacity. I write today as the first member ever raised to Cara in the present revival, and I want to tell you how that came about.

When the clan reopened in 2026 I was a member like anyone else. The first title of dignity, Cara, opens with a single act: you recommend one other person who loves Ireland for membership, and they accept. That is the whole of it. I recommended a friend I'd known since school — he had Cork people on his mother's side and had always been quietly proud of it. He joined. The Chief raised me to the honour Cara at the next gathering, by his own hand.

Two things stay with me from that moment.

The first: the Chief reads your name and title out — your full name and title, before the assembled kindred — when he raises you. It is a real ceremony, in a real house, in front of real people. The Chief's hand is on your shoulder. He says the words and commands the Herald to let it be known among the kindred that I am to be addressed as Cara Antoin, with the place and standing belonging to that rank. There is a small applause and you sit back down and supper goes on. But the moment stays.

The second: how easy it is. I had thought "recommending a friend for membership" would feel like asking someone to sign up to a service. It did not. It was telling someone I liked about something I cared about — the same thing the rest of the kindred were doing in their own ways, in cities and towns I'd never been to. Many of them tell me afterwards that they wish they'd done it sooner.

If there is someone in your life — a sibling, a cousin, a friend, anyone with a love of Ireland — who you think might want a place in the Register, the path is in your members' area. You can either send them an invitation to join themselves, or gift them membership directly. Both count toward Cara.

All my very best, in clan and kindred,

Erin go bragh

Antóin Commane, Cara of Ó Comáin
