A note from the Office of the Herald

Your name in the Register

Dear Aoife,

Your name is now in the Register of Clan Ó Comáin — both the physical Register kept by hand at Newhall, where the Chief has signed and sealed your certificate, and the public online Founding Members Register, where it stands among the founders of the present revival.

Your standing also carries with it certain other courtesies of the household, which Linda will detail in correspondence to come.

View the public Founding Members Register →

A short word about a quiet privilege of the Register. The Chief raises members, by his own hand, to three titles of dignity — Cara, Ardchara, and Onóir. The path to Cara opens by bringing one person into the kindred — either by inviting a friend or family member to join, or by gifting a €49 Clan Membership to someone who would value your present.

Once raised, the Herald is commanded to make the title known among the kindred, and the holder is addressed by it thereafter — informally as Cara [Firstname], and formally as [Firstname] [Lastname], Cara of Ó Comáin.

The clan is built for friends and family to share; the title marks those who do the sharing. Both invitations and gifts can be sent from your members' area, where the count toward Cara is also kept.

With the compliments of the Office, and a welcome from the household of Ó Comáin.

The Herald of Clan Ó Comáin
