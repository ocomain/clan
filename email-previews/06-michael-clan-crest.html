Ó COMÁIN

A note from the Marshall's office

The clan crest in your home

Dear Aoife,

Michael Commane writing — Marshall and Standard Bearer of Clan Ó Comáin. My office is the keeping of the clan's standards, the regalia, and the heraldic privileges of the kindred. I write today on a question that often comes up among members in the months after joining: what may I display, wear, or carry, that marks me as of the household?

The honest answer is: more than most realise. Membership in Ó Comáin carries the Chief's approval for the use of the clan crest and our tartan on personal items and stationery. This includes, but is not limited to:

The crest on signet rings, bookplates, embroidered linens, blazer pockets, engraved silverware, and family stationery. These are the traditional uses, and the ones members most often commission. The crest is the mermaid of Newhall Lake playing the harp — the same figure on the Chief's stamp.

Our tartan worn at family events, and at the Chief's black-tie dinner during the gathering. Members wear our tartan trousers (or skirt) at the formal evening at Newhall — the dress code for the Chief's black-tie dinner is white tie or black tie with our tartan as the trouser. Members wear our tartan also at weddings, christenings, funerals, and the gatherings. It is for the kindred, not for sale to the public.

The crest at significant family ceremonies. Many members request a small embossed crest seal for the order-of-service at family weddings or christenings — a practical and quiet way to mark a moment as connected to the wider household. The Chancellor's office can supply the digital arms file on request.

The crest on the headstone or memorial of a member. Should the time come, the household has standing approval for the crest to be placed on the headstone or memorial of any member. This is a long-held privilege of clan membership and one we honour without question.

These privileges remain in your name for as long as you are a member in good standing. The Chief regards them as an extension of your standing in the household, not a perk.

I am happy to confirm whether the use is within the household's standing approval, and to assist with anything specialised.

Yours, in service of the clan,

Michael Commane
