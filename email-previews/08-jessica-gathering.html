A note from the Keeper of the Seat

Plans for the gathering at Newhall

Hi Aoife! 💚

Jessica-Lily here — your Coimeádaí na Suíochán, which sounds frightfully grand but really just means I'm the one who makes sure there's a chair at the table for everyone who wants one at Newhall. The Chief asked me to write to you because something rather lovely is being planned and you should be the first to know.

We're putting on the first revival gathering at Newhall this summer — and after eight hundred years of the clan being scattered, that sentence is still giving me goosebumps as I type it.

Here's what we're cooking up. Two days at Newhall House on the estate. The kindred coming in from across Ireland, Britain, the United States, Australia — many of them meeting cousins they never knew they had. There'll be a formal evening with the Chief and the Privy Council (which I promise is much more fun than it sounds — there's pipe music, and supper goes very late). On the second morning, a procession to Cahercommane, the great stone fort. Music in the long room. The raising of those members the Chief has chosen to honour with Cara, Ardchara, or Onóir. And ample time at the lake and around the gardens — bring a swim, the lake is glorious in July.

Children are welcome. So is the dog, if the dog is well-behaved (and your honour will be on the line as to whether it is).

The thing I get asked most often is what does one wear? Honest answer: what you'd wear to a country wedding. Our tartan if you have it, smart otherwise. The Chief is in tweeds by the second morning, and at the formal evening it's white tie or black tie with our tartan as the trouser (or the skirt — same rules either way, no pressure on style). If anyone tells you there's a strict dress code, that's me, but only because I want everyone to feel right when they arrive.

Members in good standing get priority booking when registration opens in early summer. Capacity is limited — Newhall is a real house, not a function venue — and we expect this first gathering to fill quickly. I'll write again with the booking link the moment it opens.

I write now because your year of standing renews in the coming weeks, and renewal is what unlocks priority booking. Linda will write to you on the practical side nearer the time. From me, only the warm suggestion that, if you're thinking of coming — and oh, I do hope you are — keeping your standing intact means your seat is held.
