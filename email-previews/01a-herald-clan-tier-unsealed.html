A note from the Office of the Herald

Your name in the Register

Dear Aoife,

It is the Herald who keeps the Register of Clan Ó Comáin, and it is from that office that I write to you.

Your name has been entered into the Register, in the form you chose, and your place is now formally recorded in the household of Ó Comáin. The Chief sets his seal upon your certificate once you have confirmed the details of your entry — a small act, in your members' area — and your sealed certificate is then sent to you. Should you wish your entry to stand as it is, the Chief sets his seal automatically thirty days after your joining.

There is a quiet matter I should also draw to your attention. The Chief raises members of the clan, by his own hand, to three titles of dignity — Cara, Ardchara, and Onóir. Once raised, the holder is addressed by their title among the kindred — informally as Cara [Firstname], and formally as [Firstname] [Lastname], Cara of Ó Comáin. It is a real honour, and one many members find moving when their name is read out at the gatherings.

The path to Cara opens with a single bringing-in — by inviting one friend or family member into the Register, or by gifting them a €49 Clan Membership.

Both invitations and gifts can be sent from your members' area, where the count toward Cara is also kept.
