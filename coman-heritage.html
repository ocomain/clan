Coman - Comain - O Comain - Ireland

The name Coman and its ancient Gaelic roots

Coman was the personal name of a 7th-century Gaelic ancestor whose descendants became Clan O Comain -- one of Ireland's oldest royal lineages. All who carry the name are welcome.

Who was Coman -- and where does the name come from?

The personal name Coman is one of the oldest recorded in Irish history. It was borne by at least twelve Irish saints, including Saint Comman who founded Roscommon -- Ros Commain, meaning Comman's Wood -- in the 8th century AD, whose death is recorded in the Annals of Ulster at 747 AD.

The ancestor who gives Clan O Comain its name is Coman mac Breanan Dall, who lived around 620-650 AD. He was the son of Breanan Dall, 12th King of Ui Maine, whose death is recorded in the Annals of Ulster at 597 AD. From Coman, the surname O Comain -- descendant of Coman -- was born. Over the following centuries it became anglicised into Cummins, Commons, Commins, Commane, Cummane, Comyn and many other forms.

The surname Coman today is relatively rare in Ireland -- only 127 people carried this exact spelling in the 1901 census, ranked 3,390th. The Romanian surname Coman is a separate name of different origin. However, all who carry the name -- whatever its origin -- and all who feel the call of ancient Gaelic culture, are welcome in Clan O Comain.

Whether you carry the name Coman from Ireland, Romania, or anywhere else in the world -- if you feel the pull of ancient Celtic culture, this clan is open to you. The name Coman has been sacred in Ireland for over 1,400 years.

The chiefdom that bears the name to this day

The name Coman is permanently written into the landscape of County Clare. The great stone fort of Cahercommane -- meaning the stone fort of Comman -- stands on the cliff edge of the Burren as one of the most important early medieval sites in Munster, built in the 8th-9th century as the ceremonial capital and inauguration site of the Chiefdom of Tulach Commain.

Excavated by Harvard in 1934 and extensively studied by the Discovery Programme in 2012, Cahercommane is the physical embodiment of the name Coman in stone -- a triple-ring fort that remains one of the most structurally remarkable ancient sites in all of Ireland.

About the clan

Clan O Comain is an ancient Gaelic royal house, officially recognised by Clans of Ireland in 2025. The clan traces its documented history to 658 AD, with DNA evidence stretching 4,000 years. Membership is open to all who love Ireland and wish to protect its ancient Gaelic culture.
