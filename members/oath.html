The threshold of standing

A year and a day

In the Brehon law of the old kingdoms, a year and a day marked the threshold of claim — the time after which a thing was held to be truly yours, by your standing in it rather than by mere arrival.

The clan's convention, following the Brehon mind, is that the first year is one of welcome and observance. The new member is present at every gathering, named in the Register, but not yet voting in the clan's councils. From the year and a day, the welcome becomes a standing: you pass from Observer to Standing Member of Clan Ó Comáin, with voice and vote at the Annual General Meeting and the right to be heard on matters the clan decides together.

The Oath of Standing is the voluntary rite by which you mark that threshold. It is held back until the day arrives — both as ceremony and as kindness. When the time comes, this page will unlock, and the words of the oath will be shown for the first time.

Your year and a day
—days remaining

A year and a day has passed

The threshold is at hand

You have stood with Clan Ó Comáin for a year and a day. The convention of the clan, following the Brehon mind, is that this is the threshold at which a member passes from Observer to Standing Member — with voice and vote at the Annual General Meeting, and the right to be heard on matters the clan decides together.

If you wish, you may now take the Oath of Standing. It is a single act, taken once, and recorded on this page as the mark of your standing in the clan.

Beirim mo bhriathar — I give my word —
that I shall stand to the kindred,
and to the work of the revival, all my days.

The act is taken once. It cannot be undone, nor sworn again. The date will be recorded on this page as the mark of your standing.

Sworn and recorded
