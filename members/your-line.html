Your line in the clan

Record what you know of your line for the Craoibhscríobhaí, the keeper of the branches.

Every member's line is recorded and held by the Craoibhscríobhaí — pending the appointment of this office, submissions are received and triaged by the Office of the Private Secretary. Fill in whatever you know, however little. A single name is enough to begin. Gaps, uncertainties, and family stories are welcome — the Office will work with what you can give and write back with any follow-up questions.

III · DNA & sources
The evidence
Y-DNA applies to the paternal line. If you are female or joining the clan through a non-paternal line, a male relative's test (father, brother, paternal uncle or cousin) is what carries the line's signature.
IV · Stories
Family lore
The parts of your line that aren't on a certificate — stories passed down, places mentioned in family memory, heirlooms, traditions.

Submissions are held in confidence by the Office of the Private Secretary, reviewed by the Craoibhscríobhaí once appointed, and — where the connection to the clan line is clear — entered into the master pedigree of Clan Ó Comáin. You will be written to before anything bearing your name is published.
