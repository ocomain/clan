Members' Chronicle · 2026

The year so far

Scéala an Chláin — tidings of the clan

Compiled at Newhall · April 2026
From the Chief

A year ago, Clan Ó Comáin had been nearly lost to the record. Today there is a constituted clan, a recognised Chief, a Privy Council taking shape, a pedigree being written, and members scattered across Ireland and the wider world whose names are being added to the Register. What had been silent for generations speaks again.

To every member who joined in the first year of the revival: thank you. Your name is part of the clan's return. This is the first of what will be a yearly chronicle — what has happened, what is taking shape, and the direction the next twelve months are pointing.

— Fergus Kinfauns, The Commane
Newhall House, Co. Clare
Recognition

A clan in the Irish record again

Clans of Ireland · Under the patronage of the President of Ireland

The revival of Clan Ó Comáin in 2025 was recognised by Clans of Ireland, under the patronage of the President of Ireland. This is the formal mark of recognition that puts the clan back onto the Irish heritage-body record after generations of silence — and places Clan Ó Comáin alongside the other named clans of Ireland in the national register.

The Chief was consecrated under the old Gaelic derbhfine ceremony of Brehon succession — the first such consecration in the family's line for many generations.

Dublin · March 2026

The Clans of Ireland gathering

Antoin Commane represents the clan · Merrion Square

In March 2026, Antoin Commane represented Clan Ó Comáin at the Clans of Ireland annual gathering in Dublin — the first time an Ó Comáin officer has stood at the gathering in the revived clan's own name. This year's event brought together the named clans of Ireland for ceremonial proceedings and a chance to meet the wider family of recognised Irish clans.

It is one of the visible markers of the revival — the clan present, named, and standing alongside the others.

Scholarship

The clan library begins

Three primary sources added in the first year

The members' clan library is the clan's scholarly archive — primary sources that establish the historical record of Ó Comáin. In the first year of the revival, three foundational documents have been added:

  • Gibson, 1990Tulach Commáin: A View of an Irish Chiefdom — doctoral dissertation, University of California, Los Angeles. 456 pages reconstructing the clan's chiefdom centred on Cahercommaun in the Burren. The foundational academic work on the clan.
  • Gibson, 2008Revisit of the Tulach Commáin findings — Journal of Anthropological Archaeology, peer-reviewed, with expanded analysis of early medieval Irish land tenure.
  • Cotter, 1999Three key pages from her reassessment of the 1934 Cahercommaun excavation (Royal Irish Academy, Discovery Programme) — including the Uí Chormaic / Commáin discussion.

These are the first three documents. The library is expected to grow — place-name studies, genealogical records, heraldic sources, and Irish-ancestry hereditary-health material are planned for addition through the Craoibhscríobhaí and Ollamh Leighis offices once filled. If you hold a scholarly source on the clan not in the library and would like it considered, write to the Office.

Bloodline

The pedigree being written

Eight chapters of the clan line traced to the seventh century — and the work ahead

The clan pedigree, at ocomain.org/pedigree, now traces the Ó Comáin line across eight chapters — from ancient origins four thousand years ago, through the royal pedigrees of Uí Maine in Connacht and the Déisi Muman in Munster, the saints and scholars of the name, the chiefdom of Tulach Commáin at Cahercommaun, and the journey into modern Clare. Forty-five scholarly sources stand behind the record.

In the coming year the work turns to the modern end of the line: the individual members whose own names and places deserve to be held in the master pedigree alongside the historical record. Members can now submit their own line for consideration using the Your line in the clan page — parents, grandparents, earliest Ó Comáin ancestor, Y-DNA status where held, and the family stories that belong alongside the dates.

Every line received will be read, acknowledged, and — where the connection to the clan line is clear — entered into the master pedigree by the Office. Your line is how the pedigree finishes its work.

Looking ahead

What the second year is pointing toward

Some of the direction the next twelve months are pointing:

  • Filling the pastoral officesThe Anam Cara (clan counsellor — mind), Ollamh Leighis (health advisor — body), and Craoibhscríobhaí (genealogist — bloodline) — plus the Clan Chaplain for Spirit. Applications are being received.
  • The Ó Comáin Surname Project at FamilyTreeDNAA formally registered Y-DNA surname project is expected once the Craoibhscríobhaí is appointed — covering Big-Y 700 and the R-L21 / Irish Type III haplogroup context that anchors the male-line Ó Comáin inheritance.
  • Summer gatherings at NewhallThe Chief's home, Newhall House in Co. Clare, will be opened for the first formal clan gathering in summer 2027 — open to all members, with details to follow through this chronicle and the Office of the Private Secretary.
  • Killone AbbeyA preservation conversation is underway around the clan's 12th-century sacred site at Newhall — the ruined abbey and the Holy Well of Saint John the Baptist — to open it up to clan members and the wider public.
